# Figure Audit

For each figure or table, answer:

1. What exact claim does this panel support?
2. What are the axes, units, sample size, and uncertainty display?
3. Is the comparator or baseline appropriate?
4. Are all excluded samples or failed runs explained?
5. Are statistical tests named and appropriate?
6. Does the text overstate the visual result?
7. Could an alternate explanation fit the same data?
8. What would a stronger control or follow-up look like?

Red flags:

- No error bars or confidence intervals where variability matters.
- Percent improvement without absolute values.
- Cherry-picked examples with no aggregate result.
- Inconsistent axes across panels.
- Missing denominator, sample size, or unit.
- Baseline not representative of the field.
- Claims about mechanism from correlation-only data.

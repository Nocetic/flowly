# Scientific Review Rubric

## Summary

- Does the summary identify the real contribution?
- Is the target audience or field clear?

## Novelty

- Is the contribution new relative to the cited literature?
- Are obvious prior works missing?
- Is the novelty technical, empirical, theoretical, methodological, or practical?

## Methods

- Are methods sufficient to test the claim?
- Are controls and baselines appropriate?
- Are sample sizes justified?
- Are inclusion/exclusion rules clear?
- Are measurement instruments or annotation procedures described?

## Evidence

- Do results directly support the claims?
- Are negative or null results disclosed?
- Are figures and tables interpretable without guesswork?
- Are uncertainty estimates present where needed?

## Statistics

- Is the analysis unit correct?
- Are assumptions addressed?
- Are effect sizes and intervals reported?
- Is multiplicity handled?

## Reproducibility

- Are data and code available?
- Are seeds, splits, preprocessing, and dependencies recorded?
- Could an independent group rerun the main result?

## Ethics And Risk

- Are human subjects, privacy, biosafety, or deployment risks handled?
- Are limitations and misuse risks honest?

## Writing

- Is the paper organized well enough to evaluate?
- Are claims calibrated to evidence?
- Are limitations specific?

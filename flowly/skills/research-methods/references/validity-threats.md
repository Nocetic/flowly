# Validity Threats

## Internal Validity

- Confounding: a third variable explains the observed relationship.
- Selection bias: groups differ before treatment/exposure.
- Measurement bias: instrument or annotation changes the measured value.
- Attrition: missing follow-up differs by group.
- Leakage: training, test, or evaluation information crosses boundaries.
- Regression to the mean: extreme observations move toward average on retest.
- History/maturation: time effects are mistaken for treatment effects.

## Construct Validity

- Outcome does not measure the intended construct.
- Proxy metric is convenient but weak.
- Human labels are inconsistent.
- Benchmark task differs from real use.

## External Validity

- Sample does not represent target population.
- Lab conditions do not match deployment conditions.
- Dataset is stale, narrow, or domain-specific.
- Effect depends on an unreported implementation detail.

## Statistical Conclusion Validity

- Underpowered design.
- Multiple testing without correction.
- Model assumptions ignored.
- Effect size not reported.
- Non-independence of observations.
- Overfitting or researcher degrees of freedom.

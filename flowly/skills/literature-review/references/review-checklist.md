# Literature Review Checklist

## Scope

- The review question is explicit.
- The domain boundary is narrow enough to search.
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria are written before final synthesis.
- The time range is justified.
- Source types are stated: preprints, peer-reviewed articles, books, standards, datasets, clinical trials, patents, or reports.

## Search

- Each database or search engine is named.
- Each query string is recorded.
- Search date is recorded.
- Snowballing is documented when references or citations are followed.
- Duplicate papers are merged rather than counted twice.

## Screening

- Each excluded paper has a short reason.
- Borderline papers are retained until full-text review.
- Review papers are separated from primary studies.
- Papers are not excluded only because their result disagrees with the emerging synthesis.

## Synthesis

- Claims are grouped by evidence pattern.
- Direct evidence is separated from indirect or adjacent evidence.
- Methods and sample sizes are compared before effect claims are compared.
- Negative and null results are represented.
- Conflicting findings are named, not smoothed away.

## Final Answer

- Search boundaries are visible to the reader.
- The strongest evidence is cited first.
- Limitations are specific.
- Open questions are actionable.
- The conclusion does not outrun the evidence.
